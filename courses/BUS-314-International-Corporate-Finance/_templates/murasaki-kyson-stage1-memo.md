## Executive Summary (150 words max)

Apple Inc. (NASDAQ: AAPL) is a global technology leader generating over $391 billion in annual revenue across consumer hardware, software platforms, and subscription services. This memo proposes and outlines a structured, six-category ratio analysis — spanning Performance, Profitability, Efficiency, Leverage, Liquidity, and Du Pont decomposition — to evaluate Apple's financial health, operational effectiveness, and shareholder value creation over fiscal year 2025.
The objective is precise: to move beyond reported headline figures and determine whether Apple's exceptional scale translates into durable, efficiently managed financial performance.

---

## Company Background & Objectives

Apple Inc., headquartered in Cupertino, California, operates across the Consumer Electronics, Software, and Digital Services sectors. Its primary revenue streams include the iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables (Apple Watch, AirPods), and a rapidly expanding Services segment encompassing the App Store, iCloud, Apple TV+, and Apple Pay. With a market capitalization exceeding $2.5 trillion and consistent placement among the most profitable public companies globally, Apple represents a compelling and analytically rich subject for ratio-based financial evaluation.

This analysis covers FY2025 (Apple’s fiscal year ends in late September). The single-year focus enables a deep, granular examination of Apple’s most recent completed fiscal period, capturing the latest evolution of its services revenue model, post-pandemic normalization, and the impact of rising interest rate pressures on capital structure decisions.

Raw financial statements — income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow reports — provide necessary but insufficient context for sound decision-making. Ratio analysis transforms absolute figures into standardized, comparative metrics, enabling meaningful evaluation across time periods and peer companies. Rather than noting that Apple generated $391 billion in revenue, ratio analysis asks whether that revenue is being converted efficiently into profit, whether the balance sheet can sustain growth, and whether management is generating adequate returns on the capital deployed. These are the questions that drive investment, credit, and strategic judgments.

## Approach

This analysis will draw from Apple's Consolidated Balance Sheet, Income Statement, and Cash Flow Statement as reported in the FY 2025 10-K filing. The analysis is structured around six established financial ratio categories, each targeting a distinct dimension of performance:

- Performance:  Tracks growth trajectories and market valuation metrics such as EPS growth and P/E ratio to assess how the market prices Apple's earnings power over time.
- Profitability:  Measures how effectively Apple converts revenues and assets into net profit, encompassing gross margin, operating margin, net margin, and return on assets (ROA).
- Efficiency:  Evaluates how productively Apple manages its operational assets, including inventory turnover, receivables turnover, and total asset turnover.
- Leverage:  Examines Apple's capital structure and long-term solvency, including the debt-to-equity ratio, interest coverage, and the debt-to-EBITDA multiple.
- Liquidity:  Assesses Apple's ability to satisfy short-term financial obligations using the current ratio, quick ratio, and operating cash flow ratios.
- Du Pont Analysis:  Disaggregates Return on Equity (ROE) into its three core drivers — net profit margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage — to pinpoint precisely what is and is not contributing to shareholder returns.

All financial inputs will be sourced from primary, audited disclosures to ensure integrity and auditability.
